A tutor of one of the Oxford Colleges who limped in his walk, was some years after accosted by a well-known politician, who asked him if he was not the chaplain of the college at such a time, naming the year. The doctor replied that he was. The interrogator observed: 'I knew you by your limp.' 'Well' said the doctor, 'It seems that my limping made a deeper impression than my preaching.' 'Ah, doctor,' was the reply, with ready wit, 'it is the highest compliment we can pay to a minister to say that he is SHAKER MANIFESTO. 167 known by his walk rather than by his conversation.''

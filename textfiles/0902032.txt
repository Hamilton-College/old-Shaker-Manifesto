Dr. Warren states ' a simple, easy and effectual cure of stammering ' is, simply at every syllable pronounced to tap at the same time with the finger; by so doing ' the most inveterate stammerer will be surprised to find that he can pronounce quite fluently, and by long and constant practice he will pronounce perfectly well. Ginger-bread nuts Ginger-bread nuts. Rub half a pound of butter into two pounds of flour; add one pound of coarse sugar, and one ounce of pounded ginger ; mix all well together with one pound and two ounces of molasses; form it into nuts, or roll it out and cut it into round cakes ; bake them upon tins.

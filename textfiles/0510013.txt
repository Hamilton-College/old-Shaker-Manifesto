Cost of the late War. Mb. David A. Wells has furnished the Cobden Club of England with an essay upon the expenses, income and taxes of the United States. We copy the following statement of the cost of the Rebellion : The whole cost of the war to the Northern and Southern States from 1861 to 1866 is estimated as follows: Lives, $1,000,000; property, by destruction, waste, etc., $9,000,000,000. The gross expenditures of the United States from June, 1861, to July, 1866, $5,792,257,000. Of this the actual expenses were about $5,342,237,000.

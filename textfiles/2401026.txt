South Union, Ky. Dec. 1893. The corn crop is safely housed; an average yield. Hands are engaged in get- ting in wood for the winter, and in feeding stock. 'Weather so far has been beautiful. Ice at present only about one inch thick. Thermometer indicating 14 degrees above zero, the coldest for a few hours. Health of Society has been very good recently. The Brethren are engaged in replacing the old steam-plant at the water works with new. Our love and thanks to all concerned in publishing The Manifesto. J. H. Richardson

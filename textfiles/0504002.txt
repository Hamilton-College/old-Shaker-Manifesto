An eminent Hindoo skeptic once told what he believed to be true in Exeter Hall, London, when he said that the part of India where Christianity had never entered was a precinct happy in domestic relations and every thing that can make heathenism home, while another portion was a blighted, un happy region, because Christianity and whisky were there. There are few things in this world so utterly contemptible as contempt. It is the vice of vanity, and is a sensation unknown to true greatness.

In a late number of the New Haven Evening Register, we find the following paragraph : It is said that there is not now living a single descendant in the male line of Chaucer, Shakspeare, Spencer, Milton, Cowley, Butler, Dryden,Pope, Cowper, Goldsmith, Scott, Byron, Moore, Sir Philip Sidney, Sir Walter Raleigh, Drake, Cromwell, Hampden, Monk, Marlborough, Peterborough, Nelson, Stafford, Ormond, Clarendon, Addison, Swift, Johnson, Walpole, Bollingbroke, Chatham, Pitt, Fox, Burke, Grattan, Canning, Bacon, Locke, Newton, Davy, Hume, Gibbon, Macaulay, Hogarth, Sir Joshua Reynolds, David Garrick, John Kemble or Edmund Kean. There is a law under which the procreative forces pass from the back brain to the intellectual and turn from the intellectual to the spiritual faculties.

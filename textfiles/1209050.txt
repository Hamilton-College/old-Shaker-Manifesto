Go into such from the fresh air an hour after the company has assembled. How heavy the air with personal affluvia and rebreathedbreath. Pah! these delicate women have no thought of what they are doing. Offer such an one a cup of ditch water; would she drink it? Would she drink it if it contained even a speck? No. And yet think of it: in unventilated rooms and crowded assemblies, we inhale dirty air—air which has washed out other lungs than ours, some of them probably in 11 state of disease ! Such air contains personal impurities, particles from the lungs, uncleanly odors. Such air drawn into sensitive lungs—lungs which are only waiting to spring into inflamed conditions—creates disease. God gives us pure air. Is it not a sin and a shame that we do not keep it pure? Dr. Hamilton Osgood

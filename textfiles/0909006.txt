UNPARDONABLE SIN F. W. EVANS Sin against law is one thing; there is the letter in black and white, and there is the penalty. When the penalty is paid the sin is forgiven. Sin against conscience—religious conviction—is another thing, and is not to be condoned or forgiven by law-makers in this world, nor in the next world by the same law-makers. It is a sin against the holy spirit of truth itself. Death to, in separation from the sinning spirit—change of heart producing 198 THE SHAKER MANIFESTO. change of character—can alone free the soul from its blighting effect. Then the unpardonable sin goes with the old man, who with his ungodly deeds has been put off.

The secret of preserving eggs is... The secret of preserving eggs is in excluding the air and sealing the pores of the shell. This may be done by dipping the eggs in melted tallow and afterward packing them in bran, layer upon layer, covering the uppermost well with bran. Or salt may be used instead of bran ; or water saturated with lime and salt is also good. An English lady, an experienced poultry breeder, has preserved eggs in this solution, keeping them for several years, without a single failure.

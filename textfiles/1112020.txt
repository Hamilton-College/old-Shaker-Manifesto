TWO SERMONS Recently, Sir Garnet Wolseley, of the British army, has made himself more renowned than ever, by giving his testimony in favor of total abstinence by soldiers from in;oxicating beverages, either in camp or on the battle-field. Thus endeth THE SHAKER MANIFESTO. 277 sermon first, as testimony- against intemperance. Our illustrious contemporary — the New York Commercial Advertiser— commenting on the above, thinks, that so far as camp-life goes, the absence of intoxicants is best; but on the battle-field, a man needs all the whisky he can drink to put sufficient devil into Mm to make him willing to massacre his unprovoked fellow-beings. Thus endeth sermon two.

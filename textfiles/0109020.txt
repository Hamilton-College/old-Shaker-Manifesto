Groveland, N. Y. Groveland.—The ground, blackened by the late fire, is already improved by better buildings than those burned. Much regret is felt that the beautiful trees destroyed cannot be restored, only by years of patience. ' So God works through the ages—when the old disappears, something better takes its place. ' Be ye followers of God, dear children.' ' W. H. B. No one wishes Groveland a streak of good luck, more than The Shaker.

It is becoming the public opinion of the civilized world that Nations cannot afford to submit their differences to the costly arbitrament of the sword, that nothing is gained by a war of classes to compensate the loss caused by the disturbance of all industrial relations and free institutions. It is a shame to humanity to require men under certain 27O circumstances to fire their own houses, shoot their own father or brother, to meet those whom they have never known or wronged to fight out the quarrels which others have made. (Penn.) Governor Hartranft's Message

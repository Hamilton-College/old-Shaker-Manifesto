The reception of pure atomospheric air into The reception of pure atmospheric air into the lungs vitalizes the blood, and fits it to subserve various purposes in the animal economy. The vitality of the external tissues, however, are dependent, not immediately and directly on the lungs, but on the air which is taken up by the capillaries. No matter how large a man's lungs might be, if his skin were varnished he would suffer very much and soon die.

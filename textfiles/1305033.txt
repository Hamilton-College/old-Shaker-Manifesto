I am well pleased with 'The Manifesto.'' I cannot see that... North Union, Ohio, Mar. 1882 I am well pleased with 'The Manifesto.'' I cannot see that it has suffered any Joss in the change of its location, or in its several departments. I cannot suggest any improvement. Many thanks are due the former Editor and Publisher for the able manner in which they conducted the paper. There is no part of our little paper more interesting and instructive to me than the biographical sketches of the first founders of Shakerism. They will bear reading more than once. J. S. Prescott

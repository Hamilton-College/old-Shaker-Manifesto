Salvation Army Salvation Army. Wm. Booth or Gen. Booth was formerly a Methodist Minister, who began his work 17 years ago in the streets of London, England. He has to-day an army of 15000 privates, 760 officers, 320 corps, and an annual income of $350,000. They hold over 6,000 services weekly. The army is molded after that of Loyola, and renders unconditional obedience to its commander. The Salvation Army publishes two papers. The, War Cry for adults, and the Little Soldier for children. It is said that they circulate 360,000 copies per week.

Russia leather is made in Connecticut, Bordeaux wine is manufactured on Kelley's Island, French lace is woven in New York, Italian marble is dug in Kentucky, Marseilles linen is produced in Massachusetts, English cassimere is made in New Hampshire, Parisian art work comes from a shop in Boston, Spanish mackerel are caught on the New Jersey coast, and Havana cigars are xrolled by the million in Detroit. And for practical purposes it is of small account. 'A rose by any other name,' etc. But if one is troubled by a notion that things are only valuable if genuine—that shams are inherently and forever wrong and annoying—why then he must cultivate exceeding shrewdness, and nqt hope to get things 'cheap.'

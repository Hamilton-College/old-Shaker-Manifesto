THE MAJORITY Our friend and companion, Philip Smith, of Shakers, N. Y., sometimes preaches short, but pithy sermons, amid his financial duties as a trustee. Recently, he happened where certain fashionable gew-gaws of a wedding or else were being prepared, and was accosted by the man-milliner or other, with, ' You Shakers don't believe in such things, but I am glad the majority have decided against you ! ' 'That's so,' said Philip, ' I believe the Good Book does mention the way decided upon by the majority, and also what became of them eventually ! ''

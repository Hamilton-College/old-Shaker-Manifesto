A good Quaker, eighty-five years of age, whom no one had ever heard speak a cross word.was asked by a young man how he had been able through the trials and perplexities of a long life to keep always so pleasant. He replied, ' If thee never allows thy voice to rise, thee won't ever be likely to get very angry.'

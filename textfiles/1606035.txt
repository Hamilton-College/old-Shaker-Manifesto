WEBSTER'S ADVICE. Daniel Webster, about three years before his death, wrote the following good advice to his grandson: 'You cannot learn without your own efforts. All the teachers in the world cannot make a scholar of you if you do not apply yourself with all your might. Be of good character and behavior, a boy of strict truth and honor and conscience in all things. ' liemember thy Creator in the days of thy youth. ' God has given you a mind and faculties, and He will surely call you to account. ' Honor and obey your parents ; be patient under restraint; look forward constantly to your approaching manhood; and put off every day all that is frivolous and childish. ' ' Selected.

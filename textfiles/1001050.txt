Home hints Home Hints.— That boiling water will remove tea-stains and many fruit-stains; pour the water through the stain and thus prevent it from spreading over the fabric. That ripe tomatoes will remove ink and other stains, from white cloth ; also from the hands. That a teaspoonful of turpentine boiled with white clothes will aid the whitening process. That boiled starch ismuch improved by the addition of a little spermacetti or a little salt, or both, or a little gum arabic dissolved. That beeswax and salt will make flat-irons as clean and smooth as glass ; tie a lump of wax in a rag and keep it for that purpose ; when irons are hot, rub them with the wax rag, then scour with a paper or rag sprinkled with salt. That kerosene will' soften boots or shoes which have been hardened by water, and render them as pliable as when new. That kerosene will make tin tea-kettles as bright as new; saturate a woolen rag and rub with it; it will also remove stains from clean varnished furniture.— [Montreal Star.

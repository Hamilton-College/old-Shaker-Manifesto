Issachar Bates, Sen., was getting his horse shod at a smith's, in a western State, when he, as usual, opened a sermon at man's fallen fleshly nature, and assured the company present that Christ had come again, and that he was a representative of the new gospel. He then showed the necessity of a man's confessing his sins, and living a new life like Jesus, and lie should then be enabled to do greater works than even Jesus.

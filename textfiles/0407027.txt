The Pythagorean Hierocles said, ' there is nothing more worth seeing than the Brahmins of India, a people addicted to philosophy, who eat no manner of flesh, who live almost entirely in the open air, who have all things in common, who maintain continence, who cultivate the truth, who wear clothes made of linen, and who when eating adapt their motions to music.'

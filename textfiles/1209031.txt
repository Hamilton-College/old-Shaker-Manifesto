JUVENILE FABLE. The Wolf and the Crane A wolf having a bone stuck in his throat, hired a crane, for a large sum, to put her head into his throat and draw out the bone. When the Crane had extracted the bone, and demanded the promised payment, the Wolf, grinning and grinding his teeth, exclaimed: 'Why, you have surely already a sufficient recompense, in having been permitted to draw out your head in safety from the month and jaws of a wolf.' In serving the wicked, expect no reward, and be thankful if you escape injury for your pains. Aesop

TRYING TO BE A CHRISTIAN! If there is any truth in the following story, it ought to put to the blush about ninety-nine per cent of us who make Christianity a profession, but who love too well to retaliate real or imaginary wrongs done us, that we retain scarcely the smell of a Christian about us ! Can it be true ? ' The history of modern missions tells us of a negro in the West Indies who asked his master to buy an old, sickly man from a slave-ship, carried him to his own hut, gave him his own bed, cooked his food and nursed him till he died. When asked the reason of his conduct he said : ' I am trying to be a Christian ; that was the man who stole me from my mother in Africa and sold me into slavery.''

Home Topics TOMATOES Stewed tomatoes Stewed Tomatoes. — To stew tomatoes properly they should be cooked at least two hours; pour boiling water over them and remove the skin ; then slice them into a stewpan — porcelain is the best, although new tin will answer ; allow them to boil briskly for twenty minutes, then remove to the back of the range to simmer slowly until required ; season liberally with butter, salt and pepper. They will then be cooked to a thick, creamy consistency with few lumps and a high, fine flavor, and will not, therefore, need flour or cracker crumbs, which to our taste are no improvement. When molasses is used in cooking... When molasses is used in cooking it is an improvement to boil and skim it before you use it. It takes out the unpleasant, raw taste, and makes it almost as good as sugar. Spiced Grapes .—Eight quarts of seeded grapes, two ounces of ground cloves, two of cinnamon, three and one-half pounds of sugar. Boil two hours.

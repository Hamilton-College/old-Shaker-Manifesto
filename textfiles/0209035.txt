At the great religious revival, jn Kentucky, many j'ears ago, it will be remembered that a little boy was spiritually wrought upon to speak with much feeling and eloquence. Closing an appeal to sinners, with the dropping of his handkerchief, wet with tears and perspiration, he exclaimed: 'Thus will you drop, sinners, into hell, unless you repent! ' This little boy is now the very reverend John Rankin, who has told the story often, to many, without in the least inferring that he was once that little boy. *

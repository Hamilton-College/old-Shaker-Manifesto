Books and Papers The Phrenological Journal and Science of Health The Phrenological Journal and Science of Health, for December has portraits of several notable people of to-day, and interesting articles illustrative of their life and character. Thomas C. Mendenhall, the President of the American Association of Science; Mrs. M. E. H. G. Dow, ex-president of a Eailroad in Dover, N. H. George M. Pullman, the originator of the Pullman Palace Car. Many other articles of valuable information may be found in this number. The department of Science of Health has an excellent article on Temperance and Reform and is well worth a careful study. Fowler &amp; Wells Co., 775 Broadway, N. Y. The Philadelphia Musical Journal The Philadelphia Musical Journal for December has a portrait of Addie Reed Fleming, the contralto Soloist; of Emma Juch, who has in possession that even quality of voice, which, combined with bird-like execution, intelligent phrasing and charming manners contributes to her popularity; of Marie Halton and of Pauline Hall. Open Questions will be studied with interest by the readers of the Journal, as it is to be a free discussion of subjects of great importance to musicians. Other valuable contributions will be found in the number for December. Gould &amp; Woolley, 1416 Chestnut St., Philadelphia, Pa.

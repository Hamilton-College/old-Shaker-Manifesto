Dr. Charles Drysdale has written a very sharp reply to Herbert Spencer's saying that the ' arrest of evolution' in the female brain is the cost she has to pay for the functions of maternity. ' Whenever,' writes Dr. Drysdale, ' science has decided that women are nobler, happier, more useful, and less apt to deluge the world with excessive numbers of children, when educated and enfranchised, than when ignorant and tied to domestic servitude, I admit that evolution has brought about the time for us to insist on the enfranchisement and education of the sex.' So it seems to us.

East Canterbury N. H. Nov. 1896. Among the pleasant things to note is the presence of Elder Calvin Reed of Mt. Lebanon, N. Y. Having spent a few days in the Society at Enfield, N. H., he has called to see his gospel friends in Canterbury. We learn that 1160 bushels of apples have been prepared for the kiln. This affords about 200 bushels of dried apples. Our school is in session and twentyseven pupils are present. Henry C. Blinn.

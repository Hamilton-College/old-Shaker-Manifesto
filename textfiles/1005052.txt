In the French revolution it is said the republicans found twelve silver images of the apostles in a Catholic church, where they had probably stood a long time; but they were to stand there no longer. They were melted and coined into money, that they might, like the living apostles and their Master, ' go about doing good.' There are many in the churches like those images. We wish they might be melted, and put into circulation.
